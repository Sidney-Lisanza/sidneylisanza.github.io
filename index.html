About

I’m a computational biochemist and machine learning scientist with a PhD in Biochemistry from the University of Washington, where I trained under David Baker at the Institute for Protein Design.

My work sits at the interface of science and science fiction. I like pushing the frontiers of biomolecular design by leveraging the latest advances in machine learning.

At Prescient Design, I work with collaborators across the Roche/Genentech organization to translate therapeutic goals into model requirements. I have worked on latent generative models for co-sequence structure design, trained large language models for protein design, and contributed to active pipeline projects. This work has led to several forthcoming manuscripts.

At the Baker Lab within the Institute for Protein Design, I developed deep learning methods for functional site scaffolding, where we introduced constrained hallucination, a precursor to frameworks like BindCraft, as well as sequence diffusion, where we showed that sequence-structure pairs can be guided toward desired properties such as proteins adopting multiple states. This work led to publications in Science, Nature Biotechnology, and beyond.
